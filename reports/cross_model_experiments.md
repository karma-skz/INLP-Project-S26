## What Helps Our Story

- `gpt2-small` shows higher median SGR in failures than successes (48.13 vs 24.83), which supports the retrieval-over-inhibition story.
- `gpt2-small` improves under head amplification: failure rate drops from 10.6% to 5.5% at scale 4.00.
- `pythia-160m` improves under head amplification: failure rate drops from 7.8% to 3.4% at scale 4.00.
- `gpt2-small` has a positive mean patching effect, strongest for resid at layer 9 (Δ logit +3.691).
- `pythia-160m` has a positive mean patching effect, strongest for resid at layer 3 (Δ logit +5.338).
- `gpt2-small` rarely produces failure cases with SGR <= 1 (0.0%), so the metric usually points in the right direction for failures.
- `pythia-160m` rarely produces failure cases with SGR <= 1 (0.0%), so the metric usually points in the right direction for failures.

## What Weakens Our Story

- `gpt2-small` still has many successful suppressions with SGR > 1 (56.1% of successes), so SGR is a useful trend metric, not a clean decision rule.
- `pythia-160m` still has many successful suppressions with SGR > 1 (94.0% of successes), so SGR is a useful trend metric, not a clean decision rule.
- `gpt2-small` is not fully rescued by amplification; even the best scale leaves a 5.5% failure rate.
- `pythia-160m` is not fully rescued by amplification; even the best scale leaves a 3.4% failure rate.
- Model differences remain large: `gpt2-small` fails more often than `pythia-160m` (11.4% vs 9.0%), so the story is not equally strong across architectures.

## Notes

- No graphs are generated by this report. The evidence is intentionally reduced to tables and concise observations.
- The strongest intervention evidence comes from dataset-level head amplification and activation patching, both run on capped subsets for tractability.
- Pair filtering still depends on single-token targets for each tokenizer, so sample counts differ by model.
